Research Abstract

Occupant-centric control for thermal environments

My doctoral research focuses on occupant-centric control for indoor thermal environments, covering the full pipeline from sensing and thermal sensation modeling to control algorithm development and real-world deployment.

I investigate the relationship between exposed skin temperature and thermal sensation under both steady and transient conditions, develop individualized thermal sensation models with online updating, and build low-cost non-contact pipelines for real-time estimation using low-resolution thermal imaging.

On the control side, I design coordinated control strategies for thermostats and local fans based on personalized models, and further integrate large language models into the control framework with system deployment and experimental validation. Collaboration with Midea also gave me access to valuable real-world air-conditioner usage data, which shaped my interest in occupant behavior.
